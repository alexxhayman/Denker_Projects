## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The South African asset management market is valued at ZAR 15.88 trillion as of 2025, with **offshore managers dominating at 64.07% market share** despite being outnumbered by local managers (181 vs 230). The market shows high inequality (Gini: 0.912) but relatively low concentration (HHI: 655), with the top 20 managers controlling 77.93% of assets.

**Key Finding:** Massive capital flows to offshore managers - ZAR 1.85T vs ZAR 83B to local managers over 2021-2025.

## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. **Offshore Dominance Accelerating**
- Offshore managers captured ZAR 1.85T in flows vs ZAR 83B for local managers
- BlackRock alone received more flows (+ZAR 1.37T) than all local managers combined
- Capital flight from SA to offshore platforms is the dominant trend

### 2. **Local Manager Resilience Despite Outflows**
- Top local managers (Allan Gray, Coronation, Ninety One, Sanlam) all experienced outflows
- Yet they grew AUM through strong investment performance
- Suggests local managers deliver returns but investors prefer offshore platforms

### 3. **Market Concentration Risk is Low**
- HHI of 655 indicates healthy competition
- Top 5 control 47%, which is moderate concentration
- However, Gini of 0.912 shows extreme inequality in asset distribution

### 4. **PSG: Local Success Story**
- Only major local manager with strong positive flows (+ZAR 60B)
- 24% 5-year CAGR, 19% YoY growth
- Demonstrates local managers can compete if positioned correctly

### 5. **Fundsmith Collapse**
- Lost ZAR 337B in outflows (69% of current AUM)
- AUM down 32% in 2024-2025 alone
- Largest redemption story in the dataset
